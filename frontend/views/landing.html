About Sepsis

Sepsis is a life-threatening illness caused by your bodyâ€™s response to an infection.

Sepsis develops when the chemicals the immune system releases into the bloodstream to fight an infection cause inflammation throughout the entire body instead.

There are more than 1.5 million cases of sepsis each year, according to the Centers for Disease Control and Prevention (CDC).

Prevention

Staying up to date on your vaccinations. Get vaccinated for the flu, pneumonia, and other infections.

Practicing good hygiene. This means practicing proper wound care, handwashing, and bathing regularly.

Getting immediate care if you develop signs of infection. Every minute counts when it comes to sepsis treatment. The sooner you get treatment, the better the outcome.

Cure

Antibiotics via IV to fight infection

Vasoactive medications to increase blood pressure

Insulin to stabilize blood sugar

Corticosteroids to reduce inflammation

Painkillers



How do we contribute to tackling Sepsis



Prediction of onset time of sepsis

Traditional pathological reports take around 6 hours to confirm if the patient is affected by Sepsis or not.

6 hours worth of time gets reduced for a patients' chances of survival as the infection damages most of the body by then.

Our carefully curated data model takes in the input which is the same as required by any testing lab. It immediately provides an accurate result thus saving valuable patient time thereby increasing the chances of survival.
